1. Program Overview & Educational Goals

1.1. Introduction

This major has been newly designed for international students in response to the global spread of K-culture and the growing demand for specialists in Korean language education. It aims to cultivate global experts in Korean language education who can lead the AI era, through a carefully structured curriculum and strategically designed learning experiences.

1.2. Official Program Description & Educational Goals

The Major in Korean Language Education & Korean Culture aims to nurture students who possess a balanced combination of theoretical knowledge in teaching Korean as a foreign language and a broad understanding of both traditional and contemporary Korean culture. In the first year, students build foundational skills through academic Korean speaking, reading, writing, and presentation courses, while also learning to use generative AI as a study tool. In the second year, they systematically build academic foundations by taking courses such as Introduction to Korean Linguistics, Korean Orthography and Norms, Literature, Contrastive Linguistics, and Korean Grammar. In the third year, they deepen their expertise in language education through courses on second language acquisition, pronunciation teaching, grammar teaching, comprehension and production, assessment, and textbook development. Finally, in the fourth year, students enhance their practical skills through teaching practicum and courses on contemporary popular culture and narrative analysis, completing their portfolio for future careers.

Educational Goals

  • To train educational professionals who, based on Korean linguistics, Korean language education, and Korean studies, are capable of operating Korean language classes and designing curricula, assessments, and teaching materials.
  • To foster the ability to critically understand Korean culture from tradition to contemporary popular culture, and to develop skills in content analysis and storytelling.
  • To establish the capacity to appropriately use digital tools, including generative AI, as supportive means for more efficient teachingâ€“learning practices and for improving academic-purpose communication skills.
  • To strengthen intercultural communication skills and practical field competence in multilingual and multicultural environments, thereby supporting studentsâ€™ entry into educational institutions and cultural content sectors in and outside Korea.

1.3. Four-Year Roadmap: Training Korean Language Education Experts for the AI Era

The curriculum is systematically structured so that students can gradually develop from basic competence to advanced practical expertise.

1st Year â€“ Foundations in Korean Studies with AI

Building academic-purpose Korean communication skills and basic major competency using generative AI.

2nd Year â€“ Systematic Language & Culture Theory

Establishing theoretical foundations through Korean linguistics, grammar, contrastive linguistics, and related courses.

3rd Year â€“ Teaching Methodology & Content Specialization

Deepening expertise in language education, including pronunciation, grammar, comprehension, production, assessment, and textbook-related pedagogy.

4th Year â€“ Practicum & Portfolio

Completing practical competencies through teaching practicum, textbook development, and the analysis of contemporary popular culture and narratives.

1.4. Recommended Student Profile

This major is recommended for students who:

  • Are interested in communicating with foreigners and in teaching Korean.
  • Wish to introduce K-dramas, K-pop, and other aspects of K-culture to the world.
  • Have curiosity about Korean culture in general, from tradition to contemporary popular culture.
  • Want to develop intercultural communication skills in a multicultural society.
  • Are interested in using cutting-edge digital tools, including generative AI, in education.
  • Wish to demonstrate real teaching skills in domestic and international education settings.
  • Are drawn to linguistic analysis and systematic lesson planning.
  • Want to build abilities in storytelling, content analysis, and content planning.
  • Hope to work as Korean language teachers abroad and gain global experience.
  • Would like to take on challenges in educational content development, such as textbooks and assessments.

1.5. Career Paths after Graduation

  • Korean Language Education Institutions: Korean language institutes at universities in and outside Korea, King Sejong Institutes, multicultural family support centers, foreign schools, and positions as dispatched teachers through cultural and exchange foundations.
  • Cultural Content Industry: K-culture content agencies, writers for webtoons and dramas, cultural and tourism interpreters, and specialists in translation and interpreting.
  • Educational Research Institutions: Research institutes specializing in Korean language education, curriculum and textbook development centers, language test organizations, and graduate schools.
  • Public Sector: Government ministries and affiliated agencies related to education and culture, local governmentsâ€™ international and multicultural service units, international exchange centers, and public libraries providing multicultural services.
  • Professional Certification: Obtaining qualifications such as Level 2 Korean Language Teacher, cultural tour guide, and tourist interpreter, and working as a freelance professional.

Through its systematic curriculum and clearly structured career paths, this major aims to cultivate global leaders who will play a key role in the era of Hallyu (the Korean Wave).

2.2. Eligibility

(1) Academic Background & Nationality

  • Freshman Admission: International students whose both parents are non-Korean nationals, and who have graduated (or are expected to graduate) from a regular high school in Korea or abroad, or have equivalent academic credentials.
  • Transfer to 2nd Year: International students whose both parents are non-Korean nationals, and who have completed (or are expected to complete) at least 2 semesters (1 year) at a regular 4-year university, or who have graduated (or are expected to graduate) from a regular junior college (community college) in Korea or abroad.
  • Transfer to 3rd Year: International students whose both parents are non-Korean nationals, and who have completed (or are expected to complete) at least 4 semesters (2 years) at a regular 4-year university, or who have graduated (or are expected to graduate) from a regular junior college in Korea or abroad.

(2) Language Requirements

  • At the time of application, there is no mandatory Korean proficiency requirement for submitting the application.
  • However, to begin undergraduate studies, students must meet the Korean proficiency criteria set by the university. Those who do not yet meet the criteria will be required to take an additional Korean language training course. (For detailed information, please refer to the official admission guidelines.)
  • To graduate from this major, students must obtain at least Level 4 on the Test of Proficiency in Korean (TOPIK), in accordance with the graduation assessment criteria.
